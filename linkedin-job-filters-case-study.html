Should LinkedIn add an "Unviewed" filter to the job board?

LinkedIn • 2025

Job Search on LinkedIn

CONTEXT

Job seekers on LinkedIn often feel stuck reviewing the same jobs over and over — this feature aims to change that. The goal is to evaluate whether LinkedIn should add an "unviewed jobs" filter to help users discover new opportunities faster.

Key Takeaway: Users waste significant time on previously seen jobs, creating frustration and reducing job search efficiency.

If LinkedIn adds an "unviewed jobs" filter, then job seekers will find relevant opportunities faster and have a more efficient job search experience.

HYPOTHESIS

THE MARKET

What can we learn from competitors?

Industry standard across job platforms like Indeed, Glassdoor, and ZipRecruiter is to provide various filtering options to help users narrow down their search results.

Indeed

Indeed offers comprehensive filters including "Recently Posted", "Salary Estimate", "Job Type", and "Company". They also have a "New" filter that shows jobs posted within the last 24 hours.

Glassdoor

Glassdoor provides filters for "Date Posted", "Salary Range", "Job Type", and "Company Size". They also highlight "Easy Apply" jobs and allow filtering by application method.

A few common themes across all job platforms:

  • Most platforms offer time-based filters (recently posted, new, etc.)
  • Users can typically save searches and set up alerts for new postings
  • Platforms track user behavior to improve recommendations
  • Filtering by application status (applied, not applied) is becoming standard

THE AUDIENCE

Who uses LinkedIn for job searching?

LinkedIn Job Seekers:

  • Age: 18-65+ with peak usage among 25-34 year olds
  • Active job seekers spend 1-2 hours per day on LinkedIn
  • LinkedIn has over 900 million users globally
  • Over 50 million companies use LinkedIn to post jobs

Primary Use Cases:

  • Searching for new job opportunities
  • Tracking application status
  • Researching companies and roles
  • Networking with professionals in their field
  • Building professional brand and presence

Our target user segment for this feature includes:

  • Active job seekers (applying to 3+ jobs per week)
  • Users who spend significant time browsing job postings
  • Professionals looking to change careers or advance
  • Recent graduates entering the job market

USER INSIGHTS

What are the pain points that job seekers face?

Key Takeaway: Users feel stuck and frustrated seeing the same irrelevant jobs — it feels like no progress.

Time Wasted on Viewed Jobs

  • Users repeatedly see the same job postings they've already reviewed
  • No easy way to filter out previously viewed opportunities
  • Time spent re-reading job descriptions they've already considered
  • Difficulty tracking which jobs they've already applied to

Inefficient Search Experience

  • Job search results are cluttered with irrelevant or already-viewed positions
  • Users have to manually scroll through pages of previously seen jobs
  • No clear indication of which jobs are new vs. previously viewed
  • Difficulty finding fresh opportunities in competitive markets

Current LinkedIn Job Search Flow:

1

Search Jobs

User enters search criteria (title, location, company)

2

Browse Results

User scrolls through job listings, many of which they've seen before

3

View Job Details

User clicks on interesting jobs to read full descriptions

4

Apply or Skip

User either applies, saves or moves on

BIG TAKEAWAYS

From this research, we can conclude:

  • Job seekers spend significant time reviewing the same job postings repeatedly
  • Current filtering options don't address the "already viewed" problem
  • Competitors offer time-based filters but not view-status filters
  • Users need better ways to focus on new opportunities

THE PROBLEM

LinkedIn lacks an efficient way for job seekers to filter out previously viewed jobs, leading to time waste and frustration in the job search process.

THE GOAL

Improve job search efficiency by allowing users to filter job results to show only positions they haven't viewed yet, reducing time spent on previously seen opportunities.

FEATURE PRIORITIZATION & MVP DEFINITION

What should be included in the MVP?

Key Takeaway: Simple toggle filter with view tracking and visual indicators to minimize UX friction.

Core Filter Options

Add "Unviewed Jobs" filter alongside existing filters. I considered highlighting new jobs or sorting by viewed status, but chose a simple toggle to minimize UX friction.

Leverage Existing View Tracking

Use LinkedIn's existing view tracking system to identify which jobs users have already seen. This ensures the filter works with current infrastructure.

Filter Persistence

Remember user's filter preference across sessions for convenience. This reduces friction for returning users.

FINAL SOLUTION

Unviewed Jobs Filter Implementation

Filter Placement

Add "Unviewed Jobs" as a toggle filter in the existing filter panel, positioned prominently near other filters

Filter States

Three states: "All Jobs", "Unviewed Only", "Viewed Only" to give users full control

RISKS & TRADEOFFS

What risks would LinkedIn face by adding this feature?

Reduced Job Visibility

Users might miss relevant jobs they previously viewed but didn't apply to, potentially reducing application rates

User Confusion

Some users might not understand the difference between "viewed" and "applied" status

Advertiser Concerns

Job advertisers might see reduced visibility for their postings if users filter out viewed jobs

MEASURING SUCCESS

A/B Test Metrics

Key Takeaway: Focus on meaningful reduction in time-to-application and user satisfaction improvements.

SUCCESS CRITERIA

  • Meaningful reduction (15–25%) in time-to-application
  • Noticeable increase in user satisfaction
  • Strong filter adoption rate among active job seekers
  • No significant decrease in job posting visibility

LAUNCH & GTM STRATEGY

A/B Test Unviewed Jobs Filter

Key Takeaway: Test with active job seekers to validate impact before full rollout.

Test Setup:

  • Control: Current LinkedIn job search without filter (80% of users)
  • Variant: LinkedIn job search with unviewed jobs filter (20% of users)

Target Audience:

  • Active job seekers (applied to 3+ jobs per week)
  • LinkedIn Premium subscribers (higher engagement expected)

Success Criteria:

  • Meaningful reduction in time-to-application
  • Positive user feedback on job search experience
  • No significant decrease in job posting visibility

FUTURE ITERATIONS

What could this feature evolve into?

Key Takeaway: Expand to smart recommendations and advanced filtering based on user behavior.

  • "Unviewed Recommendations" Widget: A small sidebar widget suggesting 3–5 unviewed jobs related to the current search
  • AI-Powered Search Summaries: After a job search session, LinkedIn could summarize: "You've already viewed most Data Analyst roles in Tel Aviv — try widening your search to nearby cities."

FINAL THOUGHTS

SUMMARY

Key Takeaway: This feature addresses a clear pain point and could significantly improve job search efficiency for LinkedIn users.

I recommend implementing an "Unviewed Jobs" filter for LinkedIn's job board. This feature addresses a clear pain point for active job seekers who feel stuck reviewing the same opportunities repeatedly.

The MVP should focus on simple filtering with persistent user preferences. Success should be measured by meaningful reduction in time-to-application and increased user satisfaction.

If the A/B test shows positive results, LinkedIn should consider expanding with the "Unviewed Recommendations" widget and AI-powered search summaries. These advanced features would differentiate LinkedIn from competitors and provide significant value to job seekers.
